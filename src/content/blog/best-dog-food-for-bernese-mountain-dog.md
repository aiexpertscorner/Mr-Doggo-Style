## Why Bernese Mountain Dogs Need a Breed-Specific Dog Food
Bernese Mountain Dogs are a large breed that needs controlled calcium levels and joint-supporting nutrients. As an active breed, they burn through calories quickly and need a high-protein formula to maintain lean muscle mass.
**Key Bernese Mountain Dog characteristics affecting dog food choice:**
- **Size:** Large breed (70–115 lbs)
- **Energy level:** Energetic
- **Shedding:** Frequent
- **Trainability:** Easy Training
- **Lifespan:** 7–10 years

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the best dog food for Bernese Mountain Dogs?
For Bernese Mountain Dogs, we recommend Purina Pro Plan Large Breed Adult. Large breed formulas with controlled calcium (1.0–1.8% DM) and glucosamine are essential for this breed. Always transition foods gradually over 7–10 days.
### How much should I feed my Bernese Mountain Dog?
A typical adult Bernese Mountain Dog weighing 70–115 lbs needs 3–4 cups of dry food per day, split into two meals. Use the bag's guidelines as a starting point and adjust based on body condition score. You should be able to feel but not see the ribs.
### Do Bernese Mountain Dogs have food allergies?
Bernese Mountain Dogs can develop food sensitivities, most commonly to proteins (chicken, beef) rather than grains. If you notice itching, ear infections, or digestive issues, discuss an elimination diet with your vet. Novel protein formulas (salmon, duck, venison) work well for sensitive Bernese Mountain Dogs.
### Is grain-free food good for Bernese Mountain Dogs?
Unless your Bernese Mountain Dog has a confirmed grain allergy, grain-free is not necessary and may carry additional cardiac risk (FDA DCM investigation). High-quality grain-inclusive formulas from established brands like Purina Pro Plan and Hill's are the vet-recommended choice.
