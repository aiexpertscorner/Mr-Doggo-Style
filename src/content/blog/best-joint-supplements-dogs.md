Canine osteoarthritis affects an estimated 20% of dogs over the age of one, and up to 80% of dogs over the age of eight. If your dog is slowing down, hesitating on stairs, or showing stiffness after lying down, a joint supplement may help — but only if you choose the right one.
The joint supplement market is crowded with products that claim benefits they can't support with evidence. In this guide, we focus on the clinical science, not the marketing.
---
## The Science Behind Joint Supplements
**Glucosamine:** An amino sugar naturally found in joint cartilage. Supplementation is thought to support cartilage repair and reduce inflammatory breakdown. Multiple studies support modest efficacy for osteoarthritis pain in dogs.
**Chondroitin:** A structural component of cartilage. Works synergistically with glucosamine. Several clinical trials show improved mobility and pain scores when both are given together.
**MSM (Methylsulfonylmethane):** A sulfur compound with anti-inflammatory properties. Evidence is more limited than glucosamine/chondroitin, but most studies show it's safe and potentially beneficial as an adjunct.
**Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA/DHA):** The most clearly evidence-supported anti-inflammatory supplement for dogs. Multiple studies show reduced pain and improved mobility in arthritic dogs given fish oil supplementation.
**What doesn't have strong evidence:** Turmeric/curcumin (poor bioavailability in dogs), collagen (inconsistent evidence), CBD (limited veterinary data). This doesn't mean they don't work — it means we don't have enough good data yet.

## When to Start Joint Supplements
**Prevention:** Large breeds can begin glucosamine supplementation at 5-6 years old as a preventive measure. The joint damage of arthritis begins before symptoms appear.
**Early symptoms:** Dogs showing mild stiffness after rest, reluctance to jump, or slight lameness after exercise.
**Diagnosed OA:** Discuss with your vet — in moderate to severe cases, prescription anti-inflammatories (NSAIDs) should be the primary treatment, with supplements as adjuncts.

## FAQ
**Q: How long until I see results from glucosamine?**
A: 4-8 weeks of consistent daily supplementation. Don't judge at 2 weeks.
**Q: Can I give my dog human glucosamine?**
A: The glucosamine itself is the same, but human products often contain xylitol (toxic to dogs) or other additives. Use dog-specific products to be safe.
**Q: Are these supplements safe with NSAIDs like Carprofen?**
A: Generally yes — glucosamine, chondroitin, and fish oil are considered safe alongside prescription NSAIDs. Always inform your vet of all supplements your dog is receiving.
**Q: My dog has kidney disease. Are joint supplements safe?**
A: Consult your vet. High-dose glucosamine and chondroitin require kidney processing, and the appropriate dose may be different for dogs with compromised kidney function.
