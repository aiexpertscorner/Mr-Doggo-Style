## Why Akitas Need a Breed-Specific Dog Food
Akitas are a large breed that needs controlled calcium levels and joint-supporting nutrients. As an active breed, they burn through calories quickly and need a high-protein formula to maintain lean muscle mass.
**Key Akita characteristics affecting dog food choice:**
- **Size:** Large breed (70–130 lbs)
- **Energy level:** Energetic
- **Shedding:** Seasonal
- **Trainability:** Eager to Please
- **Lifespan:** 10–13 years

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the best dog food for Akitas?
For Akitas, we recommend Purina Pro Plan Large Breed Adult. Large breed formulas with controlled calcium (1.0–1.8% DM) and glucosamine are essential for this breed. Always transition foods gradually over 7–10 days.
### How much should I feed my Akita?
A typical adult Akita weighing 70–130 lbs needs 3–4 cups of dry food per day, split into two meals. Use the bag's guidelines as a starting point and adjust based on body condition score. You should be able to feel but not see the ribs.
### Do Akitas have food allergies?
Akitas can develop food sensitivities, most commonly to proteins (chicken, beef) rather than grains. If you notice itching, ear infections, or digestive issues, discuss an elimination diet with your vet. Novel protein formulas (salmon, duck, venison) work well for sensitive Akitas.
### Is grain-free food good for Akitas?
Unless your Akita has a confirmed grain allergy, grain-free is not necessary and may carry additional cardiac risk (FDA DCM investigation). High-quality grain-inclusive formulas from established brands like Purina Pro Plan and Hill's are the vet-recommended choice.
