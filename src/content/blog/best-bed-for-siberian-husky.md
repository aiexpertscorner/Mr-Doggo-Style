Siberian Huskys sleep 12–14 hours per day. The right bed supports their joints and gives them a dedicated space to decompress. With a lifespan of 12–14 years, investing in a quality orthopedic bed pays off long-term.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What size bed does a Siberian Husky need?

Siberian Huskys typically need a Medium (30") or Intermediate (36") bed. Measure your dog from nose to tail and add 6–8 inches for comfortable stretching room. When in doubt, size up.

### Do Siberian Huskys need an orthopedic bed?

Siberian Huskys benefit from foam support, especially as they age. Any dog with joint issues needs orthopedic support, and preventive use from age 5+ is recommended by most veterinarians.

### How often should I wash my Siberian Husky's bed?

Wash the cover every 1–2 weeks, or immediately after accidents. Spot-clean the foam insert with mild soap and allow to fully air dry — never machine wash foam. A waterproof inner liner (like Friends Forever includes) makes this process much easier.
