## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the best dog food for Pugs?
For Pugs, we recommend Blue Buffalo Life Protection. Look for a formula with high-quality animal protein as the first ingredient and an AAFCO feeding trial statement. Always transition foods gradually over 7–10 days.
### How much should I feed my Pug?
A typical adult Pug weighing 14–18 lbs needs 1.5–2.5 cups per day depending on activity level. Use the bag's guidelines as a starting point and adjust based on body condition score. You should be able to feel but not see the ribs.
### Do Pugs have food allergies?
Pugs can develop food sensitivities, most commonly to proteins (chicken, beef) rather than grains. If you notice itching, ear infections, or digestive issues, discuss an elimination diet with your vet. Novel protein formulas (salmon, duck, venison) work well for sensitive Pugs.
### Is grain-free food good for Pugs?
Unless your Pug has a confirmed grain allergy, grain-free is not necessary and may carry additional cardiac risk (FDA DCM investigation). High-quality grain-inclusive formulas from established brands like Purina Pro Plan and Hill's are the vet-recommended choice.
