In summer months, a dog lying on a foam bed is lying on an insulating surface that traps body heat. Elevated beds solve this by lifting the dog off the floor entirely, allowing air to circulate underneath and dramatically reducing heat retention.
Elevated beds aren't just for summer — they're also excellent year-round for dogs who get hot easily (Bulldogs, other flat-faced breeds), dogs who run warm, and as outdoor beds for decks, patios, and camping.
---
## Why Elevated Beds Work
Physics: heat rises from a warm body downward through convection and conduction. When that heat hits a foam mattress, the foam absorbs and retains it. When the heat hits open air below an elevated bed, it dissipates.
Studies comparing skin temperature under dogs on elevated vs. foam beds show significantly lower temperatures on elevated surfaces — typically 10-15°F lower on warm days.

## FAQ
**Q: Do elevated beds work in winter?**
A: Yes, for the cold floor. An elevated bed keeps dogs off cold concrete or tile floors. You can add a blanket for warmth. In very cold weather, a foam bed with warmth may be more appropriate.
**Q: Can I put a cushion on an elevated bed?**
A: Yes, though it reduces the cooling benefit. If joint support is also needed, you can add a thin memory foam pad — the elevation still helps more than a floor-level foam bed.
