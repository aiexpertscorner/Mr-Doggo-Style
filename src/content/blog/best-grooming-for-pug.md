## Why Pugs Need a Breed-Specific Grooming Tool

Pugs are heavy shedders that require regular grooming to manage coat health and reduce household hair. The wrong brush can irritate skin or miss the undercoat entirely.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should I groom my Pug?

Pugs are heavy shedders that need brushing 3–4 times per week minimum. During spring and fall coat blows, daily brushing prevents mats and significantly reduces shedding around your home.

### What grooming tools does a Pug need?

Essential tools for Pugs: a deShedding tool (FURminator is the gold standard), a slicker brush for regular maintenance, a metal comb for tangle detection, and nail clippers. Professional grooming every 6–8 weeks rounds out the routine.

### Do Pugs shed a lot?

Yes — Pugs are considered heavy shedders. Regular deShedding with a tool like the FURminator can reduce shedding by up to 90%. Expect more shedding in spring and fall during seasonal coat changes.
