## Why Hanoverian Scenthound nutrition needs its own checklist
Hanoverian Scenthounds have moderate energy needs — 24–28% protein is the sweet spot. Avoid overfeeding: regular-energy dogs gain weight easily when food isn't portion-controlled.
At 79–99 lbs (35.83–44.91 kg), Hanoverian Scenthounds need joint support from an early age and bloat prevention through meal splitting. Large breed formulas have controlled mineral ratios.
The most common feeding mistakes Hanoverian Scenthound owners make:
- **Overfeeding** — Hanoverian Scenthounds are food-motivated. Measure every meal and reduce treats from the daily allowance.
- **Skipping joint support** — glucosamine and chondroitin should be in any formula for a large breed.
- **Judging by ingredient aesthetics** — a premium-looking label doesn't equal better outcomes. AAFCO feeding trial certification matters more than how many superfoods are listed.
- **Ignoring life stage** — a puppy formula and an adult formula are not interchangeable, especially in large breeds where growth rates affect skeletal development.

## How to Choose the Right Food for Your Hanoverian Scenthound
### Step 1: Confirm the AAFCO statement
Every bag should say: *"Animal feeding tests using AAFCO procedures substantiate that [brand] provides complete and balanced nutrition."* No AAFCO statement = don't buy it.
### Step 2: Match protein to energy level
Moderate Hanoverian Scenthounds: 24–28% protein is the sweet spot.
### Step 3: Check for joint support ingredients
Non-negotiable for large breeds: glucosamine (300+ mg/kg) and EPA or DHA. These have clinical evidence for cartilage health — they're not marketing extras.
### Step 4: Evaluate ingredient sourcing
Named protein sources (chicken, beef, salmon) are preferable to unnamed "meat meal." Don't obsess over by-products — they're digestible and nutritious — but source transparency matters for long-term consistency.
### Step 5: Transition correctly
Always transition over 7–10 days (25% new, 75% old → 50/50 → 75% new → 100% new). Sudden switches cause digestive upset even with a quality food.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: How much should I feed my Hanoverian Scenthound?**
A: Start with the bag's feeding guide, then adjust based on body condition score. A healthy Hanoverian Scenthound has a visible waist tuck and you can feel (but not see) their ribs. Hanoverian Scenthounds weigh 79–99 lbs (35.83–44.91 kg) at healthy adult weight.
**Q: How often should Hanoverian Scenthounds eat?**
A: Twice daily for large breeds. Splitting meals reduces bloat risk, which is a real danger for deep-chested breeds.
**Q: When should I switch from puppy to adult food?**
A: 12–18 months for large breeds. Transition over 7–10 days to avoid digestive upset.
**Q: Is grain-free better for Hanoverian Scenthounds?**
A: Not inherently. Unless your Hanoverian Scenthound has a confirmed grain allergy, a quality grain-inclusive formula is nutritionally sound and avoids the ongoing FDA DCM investigation.
**Q: Should I add supplements to my Hanoverian Scenthound's food?**
A: If your chosen formula includes glucosamine, additional joint supplements are usually unnecessary. Fish oil (omega-3) is a worthwhile addition for general inflammation control.
**Q: Can I mix wet and dry food?**
A: Yes — and it's often good for hydration and palatability. Reduce dry food quantity to account for wet food calories.
**Q: How do I know if the food isn't suiting my Hanoverian Scenthound?**
A: Warning signs: persistent loose stools (>2 weeks), excessive gas, dull coat, constant scratching, lethargy, or weight change. Try a 8-week food trial before drawing conclusions.
**Q: What's the best food for a Hanoverian Scenthound with a sensitive stomach?**
A: A limited ingredient diet (LID) with a single novel protein source. Eliminate common allergens: chicken, beef, dairy, wheat. The Taste of the Wild High Prairie is a good starting point.
