## Why West Highland White Terrier nutrition needs its own checklist
West Highland White Terriers have moderate energy needs — 24–28% protein is the sweet spot. Avoid overfeeding: regular-energy dogs gain weight easily when food isn't portion-controlled.
At 15–20 lbs (6.8–9.07 kg), West Highland White Terriers have a faster metabolism than larger breeds. Small breed kibble is sized to reduce dental disease risk and improve digestion.
West Highland White Terriers rank **#47** in canine intelligence (Average) — active minds need good nutrition to support cognitive function and training responsiveness.
The most common feeding mistakes West Highland White Terrier owners make:
- **Overfeeding** — West Highland White Terriers are food-motivated. Measure every meal and reduce treats from the daily allowance.
- **Skipping joint support** — glucosamine and chondroitin should be in any formula for a small breed.
- **Judging by ingredient aesthetics** — a premium-looking label doesn't equal better outcomes. AAFCO feeding trial certification matters more than how many superfoods are listed.
- **Ignoring life stage** — a puppy formula and an adult formula are not interchangeable, especially in small breeds where growth rates affect skeletal development.

## How to Choose the Right Food for Your West Highland White Terrier
### Step 1: Confirm the AAFCO statement
Every bag should say: *"Animal feeding tests using AAFCO procedures substantiate that [brand] provides complete and balanced nutrition."* No AAFCO statement = don't buy it.
### Step 2: Match protein to energy level
Moderate West Highland White Terriers: 24–28% protein is the sweet spot.
### Step 3: Check for joint support ingredients
Joint supplements in food are a welcome bonus for West Highland White Terriers, especially as they age.
### Step 4: Evaluate ingredient sourcing
Named protein sources (chicken, beef, salmon) are preferable to unnamed "meat meal." Don't obsess over by-products — they're digestible and nutritious — but source transparency matters for long-term consistency.
### Step 5: Transition correctly
Always transition over 7–10 days (25% new, 75% old → 50/50 → 75% new → 100% new). Sudden switches cause digestive upset even with a quality food.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: How much should I feed my West Highland White Terrier?**
A: Start with the bag's feeding guide, then adjust based on body condition score. A healthy West Highland White Terrier has a visible waist tuck and you can feel (but not see) their ribs. West Highland White Terriers weigh 15–20 lbs (6.8–9.07 kg) at healthy adult weight.
**Q: How often should West Highland White Terriers eat?**
A: Twice daily for small breeds. Consistent meal timing supports digestion and house training.
**Q: When should I switch from puppy to adult food?**
A: 10–12 months for medium/small breeds. Transition over 7–10 days to avoid digestive upset.
**Q: Is grain-free better for West Highland White Terriers?**
A: Not inherently. Unless your West Highland White Terrier has a confirmed grain allergy, a quality grain-inclusive formula is nutritionally sound and avoids the ongoing FDA DCM investigation.
**Q: Should I add supplements to my West Highland White Terrier's food?**
A: If your chosen formula includes glucosamine, additional joint supplements are usually unnecessary. Fish oil (omega-3) is a worthwhile addition for coat health and inflammation.
**Q: Can I mix wet and dry food?**
A: Yes — and it's often good for hydration and palatability. Reduce dry food quantity to account for wet food calories.
**Q: How do I know if the food isn't suiting my West Highland White Terrier?**
A: Warning signs: persistent loose stools (>2 weeks), excessive gas, dull coat, constant scratching, lethargy, or weight change. Try a 8-week food trial before drawing conclusions.
**Q: What's the best food for a West Highland White Terrier with a sensitive stomach?**
A: A limited ingredient diet (LID) with a single novel protein source. Eliminate common allergens: chicken, beef, dairy, wheat. The Taste of the Wild High Prairie is a good starting point.
