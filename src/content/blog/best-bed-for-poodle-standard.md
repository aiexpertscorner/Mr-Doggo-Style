## Why Poodle (Standard)s Need a Breed-Specific Bed

Poodle (Standard)s sleep 12–14 hours per day. As a large breed, they need a bed with enough foam density to support their weight without bottoming out — a common failure point in cheap dog beds. With a lifespan of 10–18 years, investing in a quality orthopedic bed pays off long-term.

**Key Poodle (Standard) characteristics affecting bed choice:**
- **Size:** Large breed (40–70 lbs)
- **Energy level:** Regular Exercise
- **Shedding:** Infrequent
- **Trainability:** Eager to Please
- **Lifespan:** 10–18 years

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What size bed does a Poodle (Standard) need?

Poodle (Standard)s typically need a Large (42") or XL (48") bed. Measure your dog from nose to tail and add 6–8 inches for comfortable stretching room. When in doubt, size up.

### Do Poodle (Standard)s need an orthopedic bed?

Yes — as a large breed, Poodle (Standard)s put significant weight on their joints during sleep. Orthopedic memory foam prevents the "bottoming out" that makes cheap beds useless. Start orthopedic beds at age 5–6 as prevention, not just after symptoms appear.

### How often should I wash my Poodle (Standard)'s bed?

Wash the cover every 1–2 weeks, or immediately after accidents. Spot-clean the foam insert with mild soap and allow to fully air dry — never machine wash foam. A waterproof inner liner (like Friends Forever includes) makes this process much easier.
