## Why Cavalier King Charles Spaniel nutrition needs its own checklist
Lower-energy Cavalier King Charles Spaniels are prone to weight gain. Prioritise satiety (higher fibre, lower calorie density) over raw protein content.
At 13–18 lbs (5.9–8.16 kg), Cavalier King Charles Spaniels have a faster metabolism than larger breeds. Small breed kibble is sized to reduce dental disease risk and improve digestion.
Cavalier King Charles Spaniels rank **#44** in canine intelligence (Average) — active minds need good nutrition to support cognitive function and training responsiveness.
The most common feeding mistakes Cavalier King Charles Spaniel owners make:
- **Overfeeding** — Cavalier King Charles Spaniels are food-motivated. Measure every meal and reduce treats from the daily allowance.
- **Skipping joint support** — glucosamine and chondroitin should be in any formula for a small breed.
- **Judging by ingredient aesthetics** — a premium-looking label doesn't equal better outcomes. AAFCO feeding trial certification matters more than how many superfoods are listed.
- **Ignoring life stage** — a puppy formula and an adult formula are not interchangeable, especially in small breeds where growth rates affect skeletal development.

## How to Choose the Right Food for Your Cavalier King Charles Spaniel
### Step 1: Confirm the AAFCO statement
Every bag should say: *"Animal feeding tests using AAFCO procedures substantiate that [brand] provides complete and balanced nutrition."* No AAFCO statement = don't buy it.
### Step 2: Match protein to energy level
Calm Cavalier King Charles Spaniels: 20–26% protein. Higher protein in inactive dogs causes unnecessary metabolic strain.
### Step 3: Check for joint support ingredients
Joint supplements in food are a welcome bonus for Cavalier King Charles Spaniels, especially as they age.
### Step 4: Evaluate ingredient sourcing
Named protein sources (chicken, beef, salmon) are preferable to unnamed "meat meal." Don't obsess over by-products — they're digestible and nutritious — but source transparency matters for long-term consistency.
### Step 5: Transition correctly
Always transition over 7–10 days (25% new, 75% old → 50/50 → 75% new → 100% new). Sudden switches cause digestive upset even with a quality food.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: How much should I feed my Cavalier King Charles Spaniel?**
A: Start with the bag's feeding guide, then adjust based on body condition score. A healthy Cavalier King Charles Spaniel has a visible waist tuck and you can feel (but not see) their ribs. Cavalier King Charles Spaniels weigh 13–18 lbs (5.9–8.16 kg) at healthy adult weight.
**Q: How often should Cavalier King Charles Spaniels eat?**
A: Twice daily for small breeds. Consistent meal timing supports digestion and house training.
**Q: When should I switch from puppy to adult food?**
A: 10–12 months for medium/small breeds. Transition over 7–10 days to avoid digestive upset.
**Q: Is grain-free better for Cavalier King Charles Spaniels?**
A: Not inherently. Unless your Cavalier King Charles Spaniel has a confirmed grain allergy, a quality grain-inclusive formula is nutritionally sound and avoids the ongoing FDA DCM investigation.
**Q: Should I add supplements to my Cavalier King Charles Spaniel's food?**
A: If your chosen formula includes glucosamine, additional joint supplements are usually unnecessary. Fish oil (omega-3) is a worthwhile addition for general inflammation control.
**Q: Can I mix wet and dry food?**
A: Yes — and it's often good for hydration and palatability. Reduce dry food quantity to account for wet food calories.
**Q: How do I know if the food isn't suiting my Cavalier King Charles Spaniel?**
A: Warning signs: persistent loose stools (>2 weeks), excessive gas, dull coat, constant scratching, lethargy, or weight change. Try a 8-week food trial before drawing conclusions.
**Q: What's the best food for a Cavalier King Charles Spaniel with a sensitive stomach?**
A: A limited ingredient diet (LID) with a single novel protein source. Eliminate common allergens: chicken, beef, dairy, wheat. The Merrick Grain Free Senior Real Chicken is a good starting point.
