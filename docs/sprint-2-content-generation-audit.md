# Sprint 2 Content Generation Audit

## Direction

PupWiki content should serve current dog owners, people preparing for a dog, and people comparing whether a breed, product, service, routine, or cost profile fits their life.

Monetization should come from useful decisions:

- Breed fit, first-dog preparation, daily care, cost and service planning.
- Product choices for food, beds, toys, grooming, supplements, travel and home setup.
- Service choices for insurance, vet support, grooming appointments, training help, walking, boarding, daycare and future local discovery.
- Partner and product promotion from AWIN and Amazon only when it supports a real dog-care or dog-selection decision.

## Generator Rules

- Public pages must be dog-relevant and reader-relevant.
- Public pages must not expose technical, SEO, affiliate-network, feed, tracking, KPI or generator language.
- Titles should describe a real user decision, not a data source.
- Product pages should include sizing, life stage, safety, budget, care context and comparison checks.
- Service pages should include booking terms, trust checks, coverage, location readiness, cancellation terms and when to ask a professional.
- Health, food, supplement, insurance and vet-adjacent pages must stay cautious and avoid treatment claims.

## Sprint 2 Decisions

- Keep AWIN and Amazon engines active as commercial data sources.
- Improve their output so active programs and products are promoted through useful dog guides.
- Expand content architecture beyond products into services and future GEO/local POI readiness.
- Treat old weak pages as cleanup targets for later sprints; Sprint 2 improves the machine that creates and refreshes them.

## Next Sprints

- Sprint 3: apply refined generation to selected high-value clusters and inspect examples before mass regeneration.
- Sprint 4: clean old low-value pages in batches, starting with health, beds, services and partner guides.
- Sprint 5: improve page layouts, category hubs, internal linking and partner/service modules.
- Later: add GEO and OpenStreetMap POI data for local service discovery.
