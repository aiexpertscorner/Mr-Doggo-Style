## EMAIL 2 — Day 2: Dog Food Truth
**Subject:** The dog food label trick that costs you $30/month extra
**Preview text:** Your dog doesn't care about this. The marketing department does.

---

Hi [First Name],

Quick one today — but it'll save you money every month.

Here's the dog food label trick that the marketing industry doesn't want you to know:

**"Real chicken as the first ingredient" means almost nothing on its own.**

Fresh chicken is about 70% water. So when it's the first ingredient by weight, it sounds impressive — but after cooking, it drops dramatically in the final formula. Many foods with chicken as the first ingredient have less actual chicken protein than foods with chicken meal listed second or third (chicken meal is already dehydrated, so 1 lb of chicken meal contains far more protein than 1 lb of fresh chicken).

This is why a food's **guaranteed analysis** (the actual protein, fat, and fiber percentages) matters more than whether the first ingredient sounds like something you'd eat for dinner.

**What to actually look for:**
- Crude protein: 22-26%+ for adult large breeds
- AAFCO feeding trial statement (not just "formulated to meet" — actually tested)
- Calcium: 1.0-1.8% for large breed puppies specifically
